How to Trade Online

A binary option, also known as a digital option or a fixed return option, is a trading instrumentÂ in which the payoff can take only two possible outcomes: either a fixed monetary amount of some asset or nothing at all. Payout is determined before the initial investment is made. Though the amount is fixed, the percentage depends on the specific binary options broker. The payouts are usually between 65-85%. The winning trade occurs when the trade expires, regardless of the volume by which the chosen asset moves.

A broker is someone that gives you the opportunity to trade with binary options. A trader is someone that is engaged in the actual buying and selling of options and needs a broker that offers a platform for these trades.

In binary options trading, assets are divided into commodities, indices, stocks, currencies and other financial instruments. They form the basis on which an option is created.

A put option is an option that generates profit when the underlying asset rises in price from the amount that it was purchased. If the option expires at the same price, the initial investment will be reimbursed to the trader.

Unlike call option, the trader makes a profit when the underlying asset depreciates in relation to the acquisition level. In the event that the option expires at exactly the same price, the full original investment amount will be returned to the investor.

The size of the investment on a given option.

To invest, you have to choose between "call" or "put" options for a given asset. Once you choose between call or put button, you will then be asked to enter the size of your investment and to subsequently approve the trade. You usually have the option to cancel the trade at any time before approving the investment.

A successful trade will lead to a return predetermined on the broker's website.

The expiry level is the level you've chosen based on the trading interval.

This represents the specific trading hours offered by binary option broker. They are typically listed on the website.

Assets are available to investors during the regular trading hours within local markets. When the assets are not available, then it is typically due to the fact that the markets where they are traded are closed.

Expiry times on binary options range from 60 seconds to 24hours. Different brokers offer a variety of ranges, but the most common are 15min, 30min, 1hr, 24hr.

Choosing the right binary option broker depends on your skill level and your specific needs. Some important factors to consider are the payout percentages, bonuses, customer support, as well as the reputation and regulation of the broker.

Most binary options brokers focus on assets with high trading volumes since trades are usually short term and are based on historical and economic analysis.
